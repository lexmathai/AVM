About

I diagnose the system before I touch the symptom.

Most people optimize the part that hurts. An engineer finds the load path that made it hurt — then redesigns it so the failure can’t happen the same way twice.

My training is structural. At the Illinois Institute of Technology I learned to read anything — a bridge, a body, a business — as a system of loads, constraints, and redundancy. That lens doesn’t switch off when the subject changes from a beam to a funnel.

I spent years applying it to human performance: diagnosing how athletes move, where force leaks, and how to rebuild a body so it holds under competition. The work behind those results is the same work I now run for operators — a professional-sports and Fortune-500 track record of finding the structural fault, not just the visible crack.

The difference between me and a generalist consultant is method. I don’t hand you opinions; I hand you a system you can run without me — documented, load-tested, and built to transfer. That’s the entire point. If a fix only works while I’m standing next to it, it isn’t finished.
