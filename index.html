1B Control Sale

Control sale onlyTen‑figure floorNo NIL / no voice

If you want to hire me, you can buy time through the consulting arm.

If you want me to never have a voice in this system again — to own the platform, the brand, the system, and every commercial right tied to it — that’s a different conversation.

Absolute floor for any control sale of TMI: $1,000,000,000 USD.

That is the minimum number that starts a real process for a full, exclusive buyout of the platform, brand, domains, IP, and operating system.

In plain language, a control deal looks like this:

  • Scope: full acquisition of Texas Movement International — platform, brand, domains, IP, systems, and all commercial rights.
  • Control: one buyer, one platform. No partial carveouts dressed up as acquisitions.
  • No residual rights: no licensing of my name, image, likeness (NIL), or voice in any form, now or later.
  • No ongoing role: no advisory retainer, no creative‑director title, no quiet back‑channel involvement.
  • Status after closing: after a limited, clearly scoped transition, I am fully retired and fully detached from the asset.

If you clear that floor, pass diligence, and can execute a clean control sale, you walk away owning a platform you can run, extend, or rebrand however you like. I walk away with my life, and no residual ties.

If you can’t clear it, the asset is not for sale.

This is a positioning floor for any control sale, not an appraisal or financial advice. Any real process would run through independent valuation, full diligence, and proper counsel on both sides.
