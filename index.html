About

I am an engineer who builds systems designed to work without me in the loop. My background spans performance science, AI strategy, and operations for people and institutions who cannot afford failure — athletes, founders, cities, and families.

Texas Movement International is the umbrella for that work: a platform of operating companies, frameworks, and properties that turn a single worldview into repeatable systems.

What I Build

I tend to work on three types of problems:

  • Systems for performance and recovery that do not depend on constant motivation.
  • Operating models for founders and institutions who need to get more done with fewer moving parts.
  • Interfaces between AI, human performance, and long-lived infrastructure.

My bias is toward infrastructure over inspiration, and toward long-horizon systems over quick wins.

Acquisition

Control sale onlyNon‑negotiable floorNo NIL / no voice

Texas Movement International is not available as a partial sale, licensing deal, or long-term advisory relationship. If someone wants me to not have a voice in this system — to own the platform, the brand, the system, and all future use of it outright — that is a control transaction, not a consulting engagement.

Absolute floor for any control sale: $1,000,000,000 USD.

This is a hard, non‑negotiable minimum for a full, exclusive acquisition of Texas Movement International and the underlying platform.

Terms, in plain language:

  • Scope: full acquisition of the platform, brand, domains, IP, systems, and all commercial rights tied to Texas Movement International.
  • Control: one buyer, one platform. No partial carveouts pretending to be a whole acquisition.
  • No residual rights: no licensing of my name, image, likeness (NIL), or voice in any form, now or later.
  • No ongoing role: no advisory retainer, no creative-director arrangement, no public-facing obligations, and no quiet back‑channel operating involvement.
  • Status after closing: after a limited, clearly scoped transition, I am fully retired and fully detached from the asset.

If a serious buyer clears that floor and diligence, the transaction is a clean transfer of control. If not, the asset is not for sale.

This is a positioning floor for any control sale, not an appraisal or financial advice. Any real process would run through independent valuation, full diligence, and professional counsel on both sides.
